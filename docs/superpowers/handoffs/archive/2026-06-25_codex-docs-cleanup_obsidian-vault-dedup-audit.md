Date: 2026-06-25

## Verdict

The original `XIC/` subtree cleanup found a structural problem rather than
byte-for-byte duplicate notes: two archive schemes coexisted. A later root-level
pass found the screenshot-visible issue: hundreds of XIC notes were at the vault
root instead of under the `XIC/` taxonomy.

- `20 Archived Plans And Specs/Plans`, `Specs`, and `Goals` contain source
  wrapper notes generated by one migration batch.
- `20 Archived Plans And Specs/Topic Archives/` contains topic-organized source
  notes generated by another migration batch.
- Topic folders are useful, but the parallel `Plans/Specs/Goals` layer makes
  the vault look like duplicate content even when sources are unique.

Do not merge or delete lifecycle-related notes automatically. Many same-topic
groups intentionally represent goal, plan, spec, review, validation, and
closeout stages for one workstream.

Root-import notes with duplicate `source_path` are also not deletion-approved.
They are preserved in duplicate-source quarantine for later focused review.

## Cleanup Rule

Use topic folders plus document-family folders as the canonical Obsidian
archive geography.

1. Keep lifecycle-related notes separate; do not collapse goal/plan/spec/note
   chains unless a human explicitly reviews the content.
2. Keep human-facing tracked HTML in repo, not in Obsidian topic folders.
3. Normalize future imports to use a single source metadata key:
   `repo_source_path`.
4. Do not add new notes to the retired `Plans`, `Specs`, or `Goals` buckets if
   those empty folders still exist locally.
5. Do not leave dozens of notes directly under a topic folder. Put notes under
   `Plans`, `Specs And Designs`, `Goals`, `Validation And Evidence`,
   `Notes Decisions Closeouts`, `Reports And Pulses`, `Deepresearch`, or
   `Other`.
6. Do not leave project notes at the vault root. Keep only global wiki files
   there.
7. Preserve duplicate `source_path` root imports as redirect stubs after focused
   duplicate review; do not recreate a second readable body.
8. Keep XIC cleanup manifests, control tables, bases, and migration summaries
   under `XIC/01 Indexes/Migration Control Tables/`, not the vault root.
9. Prefer curated topic/family notes over raw direct repo imports. Raw duplicate
   imports should become redirect stubs, not a second readable copy.
10. Outside `XIC/00 Inbox/Root Import Review/`, link to canonical notes rather
    than redirect stubs. Root Import Review indexes are the migration ledger
    exception.

## Current Risks

- The vault is not a product authority surface. Repo source-of-truth docs,
  status indexes, schemas, manifests, validation retention artifacts, and active
  handoffs stay in repo.
- File moves inside Obsidian are not protected by repo history. Any structural
  cleanup should avoid deletion, avoid overwrite, and preserve source path
  metadata.
- The quarantined `.html` was not identical to the current repo HTML by
  SHA-256. It was moved out of the Obsidian subtree rather than deleted.
